The Core Loop

Every run is a 30-day economy puzzle.

Draft

Every fish you buy is a card added to your permanent Ocean deck. Targeted drafting and deck-thinning are critical -- a bloated deck dilutes your best draws.

Survive

Ammonia is your run-killer. High-yield fish pollute the water. Hit 3.0 ammonia and the tank crashes -- zero income that day. Pure push-your-luck tension every draw.

Scale

Eggs buy fish. Shells expand your tank. Crown Fish cost 30-40 Eggs each -- you need to build an economy engine that scales fast enough to afford three before Day 30.

How a Run Works

Three phases. Every single day. For 30 days.

1

Draw

Draw fish one-by-one from your shuffled Ocean deck into the tank. Every draw is a gamble -- you might pull a Shell-generating Crab, or an ammonia-producing Guppy that pushes you closer to a crash.

2

Score

End the day to trigger scoring. Every fish in the tank produces Eggs and Shells plus any trait bonuses. Crashed tanks earn nothing.

3

Shop

The Hatchery opens. Spend Eggs to draft new fish into your permanent deck. Spend Shells to expand tank capacity. Every purchase shapes the rest of your run.

The Corpse Economy

Some of the best synergies in Finbred are built on death. Here is one of many "broken" combos waiting to be discovered.

Flatfish

Eat Random

Devours a random fish on entry, leaving behind a corpse. +5 Shells from the kill.

Ghostfish

Haunt

+2 bonus Eggs per corpse floating in the tank at end-of-day scoring. More bones = more income.

Zombie Worm

Bone Eater

Consumes a corpse on entry for instant +3 Eggs and +2 Shells. Cash out the carnage.

30+ fish. 25+ unique traits. Hundreds of possible synergy chains.

A 30-Day Ticking Clock

This is not an idle game. You have exactly 30 days to build an economy capable of drafting and sustaining 3 Crown Fish in the tank on the same day. Buy them too early, they dilute your deck. Buy them too late, your run is over.
